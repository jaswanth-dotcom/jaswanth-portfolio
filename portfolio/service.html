Booking essential home services like plumbing, cleaning, and electrical repairs is often chaotic and unreliable. Many people rely on local contacts or unverified apps that lack professionalism, clarity, and scheduling flexibility.

To address this gap, I designed a streamlined mobile app that enables users to book trusted home services with just a few taps. The goal was to offer speed, simplicity, and trust ‚Äî all through a clean, modern interface.

üéØ Target Audience

The app was designed with the following users in mind:

  • Busy professionals with no time to call around for services
  • Elderly individuals who need easy-to-use digital solutions
  • Urban residents living in rented flats or co-living spaces
  • First-time movers unfamiliar with local service providers

üé® Design Process & Research

Before designing, I studied apps like UrbanClap, Housejoy, and Sulekha to identify common UX flaws: too many steps, unclear pricing, and overwhelming options.

My goal was to fix those issues by simplifying the flow and using recognizable icons and larger touch areas to help users quickly find what they need without confusion or hesitation.

üß† UX Strategy

The app focused on these goals:

  • Simple layout with minimal distractions
  • Quick 3-step booking flow: browse ‚Üí book ‚Üí confirm
  • Icon-based navigation for easy browsing
  • Mobile-first responsive design

üíª Design Execution

All final UI and prototyping were done in Figma. I used a deep blue (#1E90FF) to represent trust and professionalism. The app used the Inter font for its readability and modern aesthetics. Each service category had its own custom icon and color-coded card for fast recognition.

üì± Key Screens

  • Home Screen: Displays all services with category cards
  • Service Detail Page: Shows service info, pricing, availability
  • Booking Page: Select date/time/address, confirm the order
  • My Bookings: View or manage current and past services
  • Profile: Personal info, saved addresses, and payment options

üß© Prototype

Challenge: Keeping the app simple for all user types (especially elderly users)

Solution: I focused on large buttons, fewer fields, clear CTAs, and logical grouping. I also limited the feature set to reduce cognitive load during the booking process.

Challenge: Managing design scope and avoiding feature creep

Solution: I prioritized the ‚Äúcore 3‚Äù features first (browse ‚Üí book ‚Üí manage) and noted advanced features (ratings, chats, referral system) for future iterations.
